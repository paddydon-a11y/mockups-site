Website Improvement PlanPrepared June 2026

Getting your website bringing in more work.

You've already got the brand, the reviews and 20 years of trade behind you. Here's how we'd improve the website so it starts winning you enquiries from Google, not just word of mouth.

Right now the site is built as a single page, so when someone searches "plumber in Brighton" or "bathroom fitter Hove", Google doesn't have much to show them. None of this is a criticism of the work you do. It's all fixable, and these are the changes that would make the biggest difference.

What we'd improve, and why it matters to you

Build a proper page for each service and area Biggest win

A page each for Plumbing, Bathrooms, Carpentry and Building, plus pages for the towns you cover. That's what lets you rank for "bathroom fitter Hove", "emergency plumber Brighton" and the rest, instead of one page trying to do everything.

Give every page a clear Google title

The headline Google shows in its results should say what you do and where, for example "Plumber, Carpenter & Builder in Brighton & Hove". At the moment it doesn't, so you're easy to miss.

Add your business details for Google & the map

We'd add the behind-the-scenes information that tells Google your name, address, hours, area and star rating, and link your Google Business Profile, so your reviews and map listing can show up in the results.

Make it load faster on a phone

The current homepage loads a lot of extra code before a single photo appears. We'd rebuild it light and quick, which both Google and your customers reward.

Set it to British English and tidy the wording

Small touches like "taps" instead of "faucets" and a UK setting help local customers and Google trust that you're a Sussex business.

Add a sitemap and connect Google Search Console

So Google can find every page cleanly, and we can keep an eye on how you're ranking and which searches are bringing people in.
