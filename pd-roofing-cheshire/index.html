Roofing built to last a lifetime

Your roof is your home's first line of defence, but it's easy to forget about until a damp patch creeps across the ceiling. We handle everything from minor repairs to complete re-roofs, working with traditional slate and modern tiles. If your roof has seen better days, we'll strip it back to the rafters, install a high-quality breathable membrane, and keep your home bone-dry for decades. Noticed loose mortar or a slipped slate? We'll give you an honest assessment and a clear, fixed price.

Modern flat roofs that end the leak cycle

Still patching up that old felt roof on your garage or extension? Felt struggles with the British weather, eventually cracking and letting water ruin your ceilings. We specialise in GRP fibreglass and EPDM rubber systems: completely seamless, tough enough to walk on, and waterproof for thirty years or more. We can install them as part of a warm-roof setup, adding insulation to keep heating bills down.

Small extensions, new builds & joinery

Need a bit more space, or starting a project from scratch? We handle small extensions and new builds end-to-end, from groundworks and brickwork through to roofing and second-fix joinery. FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Common questions about our roofing and building services across Cheshire.

How much does a new roof cost in Cheshire? +
The price depends on the size of your house and the materials you pick. A small terraced house is a different job to a large detached home. We offer free written quotes so you know the exact cost before any work starts.
Can you fix a leaking roof in an emergency? +
Yes. We do our best to get to you quickly if you have storm damage or a bad leak. We want to make your home watertight as fast as possible to prevent further damage.
What is the difference between dry ridge and traditional mortar? +
Traditional mortar can crack and fall out over time, which leads to leaks. Dry ridge systems use mechanical fixings and waterproof unions. They are far more durable and don't need the same maintenance as mortar.
Why does spray foam loft insulation need removing? +
Most lenders won't approve a mortgage on a property with spray foam in the loft, because it can hide rot in the timbers and trap moisture. We remove it safely, clean the timbers and leave the loft ready for inspection or proper insulation.
Is it better to repair or replace my roof? +
If your roof is very old and has multiple issues, a full re-roof is usually better value in the long run. If it is generally in good shape but has a small leak or a few broken tiles, a repair is often all you need. We will always give you an honest assessment.
Do you do joinery as well as roofing? +
Yes. We have our own joinery team in-house, which means doors, frames, second-fix and bespoke timber work can all be handled by the same outfit running the build, rather than being subbed out. Quality stays consistent and timelines stay tight.
