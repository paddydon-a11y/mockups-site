About the practice

Twenty-five years building private homes, mostly for the same families twice.

MAM London was founded in 1999 by Mariusz Moniuszko. Since then the practice has specialised in high-standard residential projects, primarily working on private homes and bespoke refurbishments across the capital.

Most of what we do is delivered in confidence. Many of our long-term clients value privacy and prefer that their homes and lifestyles are not put on display. As a result, only a small selection of our work is shown publicly.

The company has grown through repeat instructions and recommendations rather than marketing. It still does. We are now looking to build the same kind of long-term relationships with private clients, surveyors, designers and property professionals along the M40 corridor and around Oxford and the Thames.

A note on how we work.

Workmanship, communication and attention to detail are the three things our clients return for. Everything else, we keep simple.

A single point of contact for the duration of the project. Properly costed quotes. A site that is left tidy at the end of every day. Photographs only published with permission, not as a default.

What we undertake

Comprehensive refurbishments and smaller, bespoke pieces.

From whole-home rebuilds to a single staircase, the same hands and the same standards. We take on a small number of projects each year so each one gets the attention the client paid for.

01

Whole-home turnkey refurbishments

Top-to-bottom residential renovations managed start to finish. Strip-out, structure, services, plaster, second fix and decoration. One contract, one team, one finish date that the client can plan their move around.

02

Loft conversions & rear extensions

Adding usable square footage to family homes without losing the character that made them worth keeping. Structural calcs, party wall, building control and finishing all handled in-house or by long-standing partners.

03

Bespoke kitchens & built-in joinery

Tailored kitchens, wardrobes, panelling and pantries made for the specific room they live in. Drawn properly, costed properly, fitted by joiners who care that the cabinet meets the cornice.

04

Custom staircases, doors & trims

Hand-set stairs with curved hardwood handrails, internal doors made to match original mouldings, skirtings and architraves restored or replicated in keeping with the period of the property.

05

Bespoke windows & doors

Made-to-measure timber sash, casement and pivot windows, plus front doors, garden rooms and verandas. Ironmongery sourced to suit the build, not the catalogue.

06

Period & heritage refurbishments

Sensitive work on listed and pre-war properties. Lime plasters, lead detail, conservation-grade glazing, coordinated with conservation officers and chosen designers.

07

Smaller, considered pieces

A new kitchen island. A redrawn bathroom. A library wall. Long-standing clients sometimes need a single room properly done. We take those on too, between larger jobs.
