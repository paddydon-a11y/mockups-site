01 / What I build

One pair of hands, every kind of wall

From a new-build gable to a flint garden wall or a herringbone patio, every job is laid by Gareth himself. No subbing out, no shortcuts on the bits you cannot see.

New build & extensions

Footings to roofline. Cavity walls, gable ends, piers and chimneys laid plumb, level and to the drawings.

Flint & stone walling

Knapped flint, banded flint and brick, and dry stone walls. The traditional Hampshire finishes done by hand.

Garden & boundary walls

Retaining walls, raised beds, piers and copings. Built to hold ground and look sharp from the road.

Patios, paths & steps

Herringbone and basket-weave brick paving, brick steps and edging. Hard landscaping that beds in for good.

Repointing & restoration

Lime and sand-cement repointing, brick matching and rebuilds. Bring tired walls back without losing the character.

Feature & bespoke work

Arches, curved walls, brick BBQs and garden features. The interesting jobs other bricklayers turn down.

02 / Down to the last brick

A circle has nowhere to hide a bad joint

This herringbone roundel is cut, set and pointed brick by brick. Every perp lines through, the border ring runs true, and the bond reads clean from any angle. That is the level of finish that goes into every job, whether anyone is watching or not.

04 / The man with the trowel

Meet Gareth Dowden

I am a time-served bricklayer based in Southampton, and I have spent the last fifteen years learning every face of this trade, from new-build sites to fiddly garden features and traditional flint walling.

I work on my own jobs from start to finish. That means when you ring GD Brickwork, you speak to the person who turns up, prices the work and lays every brick. I keep the site tidy, I keep you in the loop, and I do not leave until it is right.

Most of my work comes from people passing my name on, which tells you what you need to know. If you want brickwork that still looks good in twenty years, give me a shout.
