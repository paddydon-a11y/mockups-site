The craft

A polished plaster wall is never just decoration. It is layers of lime and marble dust, troweled thin, then burnished until the surface catches and bends the light like stone.

Every finish is mixed, tinted and applied by hand, no two walls are ever the same. From mirror-bright metallics and soft marmorino to seamless microcement and bespoke decorative murals, the work is built coat by coat until it's right. The result is depth you can feel and a finish that lasts.

How it's done

From bare wall to burnished

01

Consult & sample

We talk through the look, the room and the light, then prepare hand-made samples in your chosen colours and finishes.

02

Prepare

Surfaces are cleaned, repaired and base-coated so the plaster has a flawless, stable foundation to build on.

03

Apply

Multiple thin coats are troweled on by hand, each one shaping the depth, movement and colour of the finish.

04

Burnish & seal

The surface is polished to its final sheen and sealed, protected, wipeable and built to last for years.
